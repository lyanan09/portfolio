Fragmented Diary

by Yanan Liu

A fake video diary of mine.

Introduction

A fake video diary of mine. Using camera and AR as tools, documenting small stories of my life. The stories are fake, the feelings are real.

Concept and Background Research

I wanted to use this project to get familiar with 3d and AR, so I started thinking about how to interact with these tools.

Inspired by a comedy piece by Bo Burnham[1], in which he interacts with himself as an in-game character on a controller.

The interaction doesn't actually happen, but through video editing, the audience can feel the confusion by standing between the reality and the virtuality.

There are three kind of entities involved in the AR interaction: people, devices (phones, tablets, etc.), and virtual objects. I want to try to interact directly with virtual objects across devices. Although the interaction itself will not actually happen, the audience can watch the interaction between the real me and the virtual objects on the screen.

The final work is a video of several small stories. I conceive the stories from the things around me and the 3D models I can find, thinking about what the models are, how to use them, and how to fill the real space, and how I can interact with these models. Due to time limits, it ended up consisting of the following two clips:

  1. I really like Pringles: A story about my obsession with Pringles, but I can only afford one.
  2. I think I need a dog: A story about being too lonely and needing a dog, but never getting response from it.

Technical Implementation

Selection of device:

The final form of work is a video, which is recorded from the screen of the AR device. However, mobile AR requires the use of apps (chrome/XRviewer), which capture low resolution video by accessing the phone's camera. To improve the video quality as much as possible, I tried the following ways:

  1. Recording the screen with a phone
  2. Connecting the phone to a computer and recording the phone screen on the computer
  3. Recording the screen with an iPad Pro

The third was the best. Therefore, I decided to use an iPad Pro as the AR and recording device.

Technical Development:

Started from the AR drag example provided by three.js[2].

I want two main ways to manipulate virtual objects: dragging and zooming in/out. This allowed me to place the 3D models in the right place at a right size.

The dragging solution is already there, but there is no "pinchmove" in the event listener provided by WebXR. Although it is possible to implement the pinchmove event based on the squeeze event, it is not easy to squeeze an iPad Pro.

So my final solution is to save the current object when it is touched, listen to the touchmove event, and scale the selected object when the event fires.

Reflection and Future Development

In this project, I try to differentiate between treating technology as a goal and as a tool. Previously, I often mistakenly treated technology as a goal, which limited my creative process. And also I am learning to think about how to tell stories through video editing.

Due to the low resolution of the final work, it doesn't look well on a big screen. There are two reasons for this:

  1. Limited device screen size
  2. The app accesses the camera with very low resolution.
Future Development:
  • A better title
  • More stories
  • Searching for solutions to the problem of low resolution and exploring methods of storytelling that do not rely on screen recording.
  • Consider more ways to interact, such as virtual objects respond after a person does something in a video.
